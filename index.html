Our Mission

The Society of Computer and Information Science is a project-based organization founded at the University of Arkansas at Little Rock in 2011. What does that mean? Our members create and manage awesome projects while gaining and applying the skills that they are learning while studying at the university. The goal, at the end of the day, is to get a job or earn a living after college and in the competitive world of job applicants your skills and level of experience are what make you stand out to employers. In SOCIS you will learn to work with others and gain exposure to new technologies and hopefully you will make a few new friends. SOCIS is student lead, so the limits of your creativity are only you and the club's budget of course. Have an interesting idea or technology topic you want to delve deeper into? Join SOCIS and start a conversation...all majors are welcome.

SOCIS Arcade Cabinet

A Raspberry Pi Powered Retro-gaming Experience

The SOCIS Arcade Cabinet is a retro-style gaming cabinet the was the feature SOCIS Project of 2018. The cabinet is powered by a single Raspberry Pi that is running a special version of Linux called RetroPi. The cabinet was painted and wired by members of SOCIS. The Cabinet is open for public use in the EIT Building on campus,and it has a rotating collection of games.

  • Completion Date: 2018
  • Creators: Zachary Randolph, Jacob Howard, John Yankowskas, Sunny Singh, Colby Hamilton, and Trevor Thompson

Synthepic Music Box

A Music Box that can write its own music.

When the project began in Spring of 2019, the Synthepic Music Box started off as a delve into what capabilities Python and a Raspberry Pi could be given. The music box is powered by a Raspberry Pi and a Makey-Makey contoller board. The interface of the application, and its functionality were written in Python, and a library called PyGame, by Brock and the box was designed by both Brock and Hunter. Brock also wrote a set of helper programs that can be combined to allow the music box to recreate any MIDI file you want to have it play.

  • Date: March 2019
  • Creators: Brock Butler and Hunter Wright
